Pet Health & Care Guide ❤️
Detailed care, diet, and grooming schedules for every pet type

🏋️ Exercise Needs

  • Large breeds: 60–90 mins daily walk/run
  • Medium breeds: 45–60 mins exercise per day
  • Small breeds: 20–30 mins daily walks
  • At least 2 outdoor walks per day recommended
  • Mental stimulation with toys and puzzles is essential
  • Avoid intense exercise in extreme heat

💊 Vaccination Schedule

  • 6–8 weeks: Distemper, Parvovirus (first dose)
  • 10–12 weeks: DHPP combination vaccine
  • 14–16 weeks: Rabies vaccine (mandatory)
  • 12–16 months: Booster for all vaccines
  • Annual: Rabies, DHPP, Bordetella boosters
  • Every 3 years: Rabies after initial series

🩺 Health Checkups

  • Puppy: every 3–4 weeks until 16 weeks old
  • Adult (1–7 yrs): once per year
  • Senior (7+ yrs): every 6 months
  • Monthly: flea and tick prevention check
  • Dental cleaning: every 6–12 months
  • Heartworm test: annually

🚨 Warning Signs

  • Loss of appetite for more than 24 hours
  • Excessive vomiting or diarrhea
  • Laboured or rapid breathing
  • Swollen or distended abdomen
  • Sudden lethargy or collapse
  • Pale gums or bluish tongue — emergency!

🍽️ Diet Guide

🌅

Morning (7–8 AM)

Dry kibble or wet food — 25–30% of daily portion. Fresh water always available. For puppies: 3–4 small meals per day. Adults: 2 meals per day.

☀️

Midday Snack (optional)

Training treats, carrot sticks, apple slices (no seeds), plain cooked chicken. Keep treats under 10% of daily calories. Avoid grapes, raisins, onion, chocolate — these are toxic!

🌙

Evening (5–6 PM)

Second main meal — 50–55% of daily portion. Balanced commercial food or home-cooked with vet approval. Include Omega-3 supplements for coat health.

💧

Hydration (All Day)

Always provide fresh, clean water. Average dog needs ~30ml of water per kg of body weight per day. Monitor intake — excessive thirst may indicate illness.

🎮 Enrichment & Play

  • 15–20 minutes of interactive play twice daily
  • Window perches and climbing trees are essential
  • Feather wands, laser pointers, puzzle feeders
  • Indoor cats need more stimulation than outdoor cats
  • Rotate toys to maintain interest

💊 Vaccination Schedule

  • 6–8 weeks: FVRCP (panleukopenia, herpesvirus)
  • 10–12 weeks: Second FVRCP dose
  • 14–16 weeks: Rabies + third FVRCP
  • 1 year later: FVRCP and Rabies boosters
  • Every 1–3 years: FVRCP depending on lifestyle
  • Annually: FeLV if outdoor/multi-cat household

🏥 Litter Box Care

  • Rule: 1 box per cat + 1 extra
  • Scoop daily; full change every week
  • Unscented, clumping litter preferred by most cats
  • Place in quiet, accessible location
  • Watch for changes — may indicate UTI or stress

🚨 Warning Signs

  • Sudden hiding or change in behaviour
  • Straining to urinate — vet immediately!
  • Not eating for 24+ hours
  • Discharge from eyes or nose
  • Rapid weight loss

🍽️ Diet Guide

🌅

Morning (7–8 AM)

Wet food preferred over dry — helps with hydration. Cats are obligate carnivores; high protein is essential. Portion: ~50–80g wet food OR 30–40g dry kibble for average adult cat.

🌙

Evening (5–6 PM)

Second meal — same quantity. For multi-cat homes, separate feeding stations prevent stress. Avoid dog food, onion, garlic, milk (most cats are lactose intolerant).

💧

Hydration Tip

Many cats prefer running water — consider a pet fountain. Wet food provides 70–80% moisture. Low water intake can cause kidney issues in cats.

🏃 Exercise & Space

  • Minimum 3–4 hours of free roam daily outside cage
  • Cage: at least 4× the rabbit's body length
  • Bunnies are social; pairs are happier than singletons
  • Rabbit-proof your home (cables, toxic plants)
  • Outdoor runs must be predator-proof

🥬 Diet Basics

  • 80% of diet: unlimited fresh timothy hay
  • 15%: fresh leafy greens (romaine, kale, parsley)
  • 5%: quality pellets (1/4 cup per 2kg body weight)
  • Avoid sugary fruits; only small treats occasionally
  • Never feed iceberg lettuce, avocado, or onion

💊 Health Care

  • Spay/neuter by 4–6 months — reduces cancer risk
  • Annual vet check with rabbit-savvy vet essential
  • VHD and myxomatosis vaccines where available
  • GI stasis is a life-threatening emergency in rabbits
  • Check teeth and nails monthly

🚨 Emergency Signs

  • Not eating or producing droppings — vet immediately!
  • Head tilt (ear infection or neurological)
  • Laboured breathing or sneezing
  • Wet fur around mouth (dental issues)
  • Hind leg paralysis or dragging

🏠 Housing & Environment

  • Cage should be as large as budget allows
  • Width more important than height for flight
  • Keep away from kitchen fumes and drafts
  • 12 hours light, 12 hours dark for sleep cycle
  • Perches of varying sizes prevent foot problems
  • Temperature: 18–29°C ideal; no sudden changes

🥦 Diet Guide

  • Pellets: 60–70% of diet (species-appropriate)
  • Fresh vegetables: 20–30% (leafy greens, carrots, bell pepper)
  • Seeds: 10% only — seed-only diets cause malnutrition
  • Fresh water daily in clean bowl
  • Toxic: avocado, chocolate, caffeine, onion, salt

💊 Health & Vet Care

  • Annual exam with an avian vet is essential
  • Many diseases in birds are silent until advanced
  • Psittacosis can spread to humans — keep area clean
  • Watch for fluffed feathers, nasal discharge, weight loss
  • Quarantine any new bird for 30 days

🧠 Mental Stimulation

  • Parrots need 2–4 hours of interaction daily
  • Rotate toys weekly to prevent boredom
  • Foraging toys encourage natural behaviour
  • Talking, singing, and training strengthen bond
  • Mirror toys can reduce loneliness for solo birds

How to Use PawsHome

A complete guide to browsing, adopting, and listing pets on our platform

1

Browse Pets for Adoption

Click Adopt in the navigation to see all available pets. Use the filter buttons to narrow by type (dogs, cats, rabbits, birds). The search bar lets you search by name or breed instantly.

💡 Tip: Click any pet card to open the full detail view with description and contact info.
2

Send an Adoption Enquiry

On any pet's detail card, tap "Enquire / Adopt Me". A form will appear for you to fill in your name, phone, and message. This sends your enquiry to the pet's listed contact. You can also call directly using the phone number shown.

💡 Tip: Write a personal message about your home environment — it helps the owner decide!
3

List a Pet for Adoption

Go to Add Pet in the navigation. Fill in the pet's name, type, breed, age, gender, and a description. Upload a photo by entering a URL or uploading directly from your phone/computer as a PNG or JPG file. Add your contact details so interested adopters can reach you.

💡 Tip: A good photo and detailed description get 5× more responses!
4

Use the Care & Health Guide

Tap Care Guide to access detailed information about dogs, cats, rabbits, and birds. Each section covers exercise, vaccinations, diet schedules with meal timings, grooming frequency, and emergency warning signs.

💡 Tip: Bookmark this page — it's a handy reference for new and experienced pet owners alike!
5

Find Vets & Shelters

The Find Vets page lists veterinary clinics, animal shelters, and 24×7 emergency services with phone numbers and addresses. Tap Use My Location to find the nearest ones. You can also add a vet or shelter you know using the form at the bottom.

💡 Tip: Save the emergency vet numbers in your phone now — you don't want to search in a panic!
6

On Mobile or Desktop?

PawsHome works perfectly on all devices. On mobile, tap the ☰ menu in the top right to navigate. All features — photo upload, filtering, enquiries, and the care guide — are fully accessible on phones and tablets.

💡 Tip: Add PawsHome to your home screen for quick access like a native app.
Frequently Asked Questions 🙋
Is PawsHome free to use? ▼
Yes! PawsHome is completely free for both adopters and people listing pets. We believe every animal deserves a home and don't charge any fees.
How do I contact a pet's owner? ▼
Click on any pet card to open the detail view. You'll see the contact name, phone number, and email (if provided). You can call them directly or use the "Enquire" form to send a message.
Can I upload a photo from my phone? ▼
Absolutely! On the "Add Pet" page, click the "Upload File" tab. You can upload PNG or JPG images directly from your phone's gallery or camera roll. No URL needed.
Is my information private? ▼
The contact information you provide when listing a pet is visible to anyone who views the pet's detail page. Only share information you're comfortable making public. Your location is only used locally in your browser to find nearby vets — it is never sent to a server.
How long will a listing stay active? ▼
Listings are stored in your browser's local storage. They will persist as long as you don't clear your browser data. If you need the pet to be removed from the listing, contact the site administrator.
What if I find an injured stray animal? ▼
Visit our Vets & Shelters page and call the nearest 24×7 emergency clinic or animal shelter. Many shelters will dispatch a rescue team. Keep the animal calm and in a quiet, contained space while you wait for help.
